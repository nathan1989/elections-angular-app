Terms of use for candidate content on the Election website

The purpose of this website is to provide information to voters, including how to vote and who is standing for election in their local area. As set out in the Local Electoral Regulations, the Council can publish or display candidate profile statements as submitted with their nomination on its website (or in any other manner that we consider appropriate).

This election, the Council is offering candidates the opportunity to provide some information in addition to the candidate profile statement which may assist voters in learning more about their candidates. This content is not intended to be an “election advertisement”, to provide any candidate with any benefit over another, or to apply council resources for the purpose of electing any one candidate. This is a platform which is equal for all candidates to use, and we encourage everyone to participate.

In addition to your candidate profile statement, you have the opportunity to provide your thoughts on a selection of provided topics. We have intentionally selected broad topics, where possible, to accommodate all candidates’ views and backgrounds. You may feel free to focus on a specific area of interest to you within each topic. Remember, the purpose is to introduce yourself, not to talk about others.

We ask that you are polite and respectful with your comments and views and that you focus on issues, not personalities. This means not using any threatening, defamatory, offensive, disrespectful, rude, or abusive language; making direct personal attacks or disclosing private information or details of elected members or staff or members of the public; including objectionable material; and unsolicited advertising, marketing or spam. We may choose not to publish content if it is deemed to violate any of our terms of use or the rules set out in the Local Electoral Act, as advised by the Electoral Officer. If any of your content falls into this category, we will contact you to discuss how you may choose to proceed.

All candidate content hosted on this website is also subject to the Auckland Council website Terms and Conditions. If you are unsure about any rules, including campaigning and offences, go here (PDF 9.13MB) or contact the Electoral Office on 0800 922 822.

If you wish to contact us about any content on this page, please feel free to call us on 09 301 0101.

Facebook terms of use

Purpose of this Facebook page:

  1. to provide you with information, latest news and events about the Auckland Council elections 2016
  2. to hear from you about what you love about Auckland and how we can all work together to shape Auckland’s future.

While this page is about the elections, we won’t comment on political issues or get involved in debates. This page is about promoting the Auckland Council elections to encourage voter participation and discussion. It is not a place for personal marketing or promoting yourself (or others) as candidates. The usual rules set out in the Local Electoral Act apply.

If you are unsure about any rules, including on campaigning and offences, go here (PDF 9.13MB) or contact the Electoral Office on 0800 922 822. You can also visit the Election Services website.

- Moderation Policy -

We openly welcome your comments and encourage discussion. We ask that you are polite and respectful with your comments and views.

We may choose to hide or remove harmful posts or comments from this page. This includes any post or comment containing threatening, defamatory, offensive, disrespectful, rude, or abusive language; direct personal attacks or disclosure of private information or details of elected members or staff, other Facebook users or individuals; objectionable material; and unsolicited advertising, marketing or spam. We may also remove any posts or comments that may relate to an offence under the Local Electoral Act.

If your posts or comments fail to comply with Facebook’s terms, and/or the above rules, then we may block you from posting to this page. If you wish to contact us about any content on this page, please feel free to comment or phone us on 09 301 0101.

Election photo competition

Auckland Council is looking to raise awareness of the Auckland Council 2016 elections, and is using this competition to help inform the public about the election.

A. Competition

  1. The Competition is for the best pictures showing an image inspired by Auckland (“picture”).
  2. To enter, entrants must either:
    • post a picture(s) to a publicly available Facebook post using the hashtags  #VoteAKL and #LoveAuckland and “like” “VoteAKL” on Facebook; or
    • post a picture(s) to your Instagram account using the hash tag #VoteAKL and #LoveAuckland on an Instagram image that is accessible to the public and follow @VoteAKL on Instagram.
  3. Entries must be submitted at the latest by 5pm on 21 September 2016.
  4. Every picture will be moderated and any picture with inappropriate content will be excluded and the entrant disqualified from the Competition.

By providing any picture, including any links to other websites, entrants represent that they own that content or have the right to publish the content on Facebook or Instagram and to grant the Council the right to reproduce and make the content available and publish the content on the Auckland Council Elections Facebook page (“the Facebook Page”), the Auckland Council Elections website (“the Election Webpage”), other social media, e.g. Twitter; the Council’s website; OurAuckland publication; and on t-shirts, billboards, postcards and badges.

  1. The Competition is open to members of the public aged 18 years and over.
